Types of Chefs


  • Executive Chef
    The head chef of a professional kitchen or of a group of professional kitchens.
  • Executive Pastry Chef
    Oversees the production of pastries, sweets, breads, ice creams, toppings and fillings.
    He or she is the expert of creation, execution, and presentation of pastries and desserts.
  • Chef de Cuisine
    Oversees, executes and develops menus for Restaurants
  • Exec. Sous Chef/Sous Chef
    Second in authority in a restaurant or kitchen, ranking below the chef de cuisine
  • Prep Cook/Line Cooks
    Sets up and stocks food items and other necessary supplies.
    Prepares food items by cutting, chopping, mixing, and preparing sauces.
    Cooks food items by grilling, frying, saut√©ing, and other cooking methods
  • Itamae
    Is a cook in a Japanese kitchen, or a chef in larger restaurants (esp. of high-end Japanese cuisine)


Being a Chef can be daunting task. You start as a cook or prep cook working long arduous hours for years of experience. Until, finally you are given the choice to move up to Sous Chef or maybe even Head Chef only to work even more hours; think of plans a/b/c; and have an answer to every count, question and dish. A chef isn't just one individual it is a symphony of persons that will work more hours for even less pay. But they do it because they love it. Chefs want to be creative and wow everybody but they are humans to. Humans that break, sweat and bleed. So if you have bad dish just know that call offs, delays, and broken equipment happens to everyone but the team will make it up to you. Usually with a dessert from the Pastry Chef, they know how to sweeten the deal.
